Andy Ramirez

Dream to Grow

Andy Ramirez is a young Queer Latinx artist, originally from Venezuela. They moved to the United States as a teenager and went on to study at Stetson University, graduating in spring 2022 with a dual degree in Digital Arts and Business Administration.

Their creative works have a focus on 2D and 3D animation, illustration, and graphic design. Through their art, they seek to increase the representation of minorities in media, make individuals feel seen, and provide a home within the stories, characters, and environments they create.

As part of the Bonner Program, Andy has dedicated much of their work towards social justice and serving the community, remaining passionate about issues regarding immigration, LGBTQ rights, and mental health.

Artist Statement

How do stories change us? I believe stories have the power to teach, influence, inspire and connect us to one another. Art but more specifically computer animation has the power to bring those stories to life. I am an artist but foremost I am a storyteller constantly growing and developing my craft. I create digital illustrations and animations, using a combination of 2D and 3D techniques. In the past I used to work on traditional art which developed into digital art after realizing its infinite possibilities. As a queer Latinx high school senior seeing the short “In a Heartbeat”, my path was set, to create the representation I was craving and lacked growing up.
